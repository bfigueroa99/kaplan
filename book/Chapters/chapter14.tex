\section{Reading Context}
\begin{readingbox}[title=Dialogue: Catching Up]
\textbf{Tom:} Hi Sarah! I haven't seen you for ages. What \textbf{have you been doing}?\\
\textbf{Sarah:} Hey Tom! I\textbf{'ve been working} a lot. I started a new job in January.\\
\textbf{Tom:} Really? How long \textbf{have you been working} there?\\
\textbf{Sarah:} For about six months. What about you?\\
\textbf{Tom:} I\textbf{'ve been traveling}. I just got back from Asia.\\
\textbf{Sarah:} Wow! \textbf{Have you been learning} any new languages?\\
\textbf{Tom:} A little bit. I\textbf{'ve been trying} to learn Japanese \textbf{since} I visited Tokyo.
\end{readingbox}

\section{Grammar Focus: Present Perfect Continuous}

This tense is used for actions that started in the past and continue until now, or have recently stopped with visible results.

\begin{grammarbox}[title=Structure]
\begin{center}
\Large Subject + \textbf{have/has been} + \textbf{verb-ing}
\end{center}

\textbf{Examples:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item I \textbf{have been studying} English for 3 years. \trans{He estado estudiando...}
    \item She \textbf{has been waiting} since 9 AM. \trans{Ella ha estado esperando...}
\end{itemize}
\end{grammarbox}

\section{Key Concepts: For vs. Since}

\begin{vocabbox}[title=Time Expressions]
\textbf{FOR} + Period of Time (Duration)
\begin{itemize}
    \item for 2 hours \trans{por 2 horas}
    \item for a long time \trans{por mucho tiempo}
    \item for 10 years \trans{por 10 años}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{SINCE} + Point in Time (Start date)
\begin{itemize}
    \item since Monday \trans{desde el lunes}
    \item since 2010 \trans{desde 2010}
    \item since I was a child \trans{desde que era niño}
\end{itemize}
\end{vocabbox}

\section{Key Takeaways}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Use Present Perfect Continuous for ongoing actions (have been -ing).
    \item Use "for" with a duration (for 2 years).
    \item Use "since" with a starting point (since 2020).
    \item Stative verbs (know, like, believe) are usually NOT used in continuous forms.
\end{itemize}
