\section{Lesson Objectives}
In this chapter, you will learn:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Vocabulary for different types of accommodation
    \item How to describe your living space and furniture
    \item Common housing problems (leaks, stains, scratches, etc.)
    \item How to make complaints and request repairs
    \item Describing places and landscapes effectively
\end{itemize}

\section{Types of Accommodation}

When living abroad or travelling, you might encounter different types of accommodation.

\begin{vocabbox}[title=Accommodation Types]
\begin{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Hostel} - Budget accommodation with shared rooms (dormitories). Can be noisy ("snoring") and lack privacy ("no intimacy"). Good for meeting people.
    \item \keyterm{Flat-share} - Sharing an apartment with other people. You usually have your own room but share the kitchen and bathroom.
    \item \keyterm{Homestay} - Living with a local family. Good for practicing language but rules might be strict ("children are noisy").
    \item \keyterm{Hall of Residence} (Student Hall) - Accommodation provided by universities. Often small rooms but social.
\end{itemize}
\end{vocabbox}

\section{Describing Your Accommodation}

\begin{readingbox}[title=Sample Description: My Student Room]
I live in a \textbf{student castle}, which is located in Oxford. My room in this accommodation is small but \textbf{cozy}. It has a bed, a desk, and a little toilet.
For storage, I have a \textbf{wardrobe} where I hang my clothes and store my shoes. There is also a \textbf{shelf} near my desk, which I use to keep folders and other things. On the wall, there is a \textbf{board} to pin notes and photos.
Sometimes I listen to music from my neighbor, who practices the saxophone; it is actually quite relaxing to hear.
\end{readingbox}

\subsection{Useful Vocabulary}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Cozy} - Small, warm, and comfortable.
    \item \textbf{Wardrobe} - A large cupboard for hanging clothes.
    \item \textbf{Shelf} - A flat board to put things on.
    \item \textbf{Pinboard/Bulletin board} - A board for displaying notices.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Defects and Damage}

\begin{tabular}{|l|p{5cm}|p{5cm}|}
\hline
\textbf{Problem} & \textbf{Definition} & \textbf{Collocations \& Examples} \\
\hline
\textbf{Leak} & Liquid escaping through a hole or crack. & \textit{Abundant leak, fix a leak.}\\ "The pipe has a leak." \\
\hline
\textbf{Scratch} & A slight cut or mark on a surface. & \textit{Light/Deep/Fine/Surface/Visible scratch.}\\ "The cat left scratches on the door." \\
\hline
\textbf{Dent} & A hollow mark in a hard surface caused by a blow. & \textit{Small/Deep/Minor/Large dent.}\\ "The hailstorm caused a dent on the roof." \\
\hline
\textbf{Stain} & A colored patch that is difficult to remove. & \textit{Blood/Ink/Oil/Wine stain.}\\ "Red wine caused a stain on the carpet." \\
\hline
\textbf{Rip/Tear} & A hole caused by pulling apart (fabric/paper). & \textit{Jagged rip, torn muscle.}\\ "There is a rip in the sofa." \\
\hline
\textbf{Crack} & A line on the surface where it has split without breaking apart. & "There is a crack in the wall." \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\subsection{Verbs for Damage and Repair}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{To cause/leave} a scratch/dent/stain: "The knife \textbf{left} a scratch on the plate."
    \item \textbf{To fix/repair/mend} a problem: "I need to \textbf{repair} the dent."
    \item \textbf{To remove} a stain/scratch: "I used a cleaner to \textbf{remove} the stain."
    \item \textbf{To cover} up: "She used a sticker to \textbf{cover} the scratch."
\end{itemize}

\section{Making Complaints and Requests}

When you have a problem, you need to use polite but firm language to ask for a solution.

\begin{readingbox}[title=Dialogue: Reporting a Problem]
\textbf{Tenant:} Hello, I have a request. I am staying in a homestay, but I have a problem with the family.\\
\textbf{Agent:} I'm sorry to hear that. What is the problem?\\
\textbf{Tenant:} The children are very noisy, and I can't work in my room. I tried to talk to them, but they didn't listen.\\
\textbf{Agent:} I understand. We want to make sure you have a comfortable stay. Let me check if we have another homestay available.\\
\textbf{Tenant:} Thank you, I would appreciate that. If possible, I would like to move as soon as possible, or I would like a \textbf{reduction in my rent}.
\end{readingbox}

\subsection{Useful Phrases for Complaints}
\begin{itemize}
    \item "I have a request regarding..."
    \item "I would appreciate it if you could..."
    \item "I would like to change/move..."
    \item "There seems to be a problem with..."
    \item "Could you please send an electrician/plumber?"
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Common Repairs}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Fix a leak

    \item Change the lighting
    \item Replace broken tiles
    \item Remodel the kitchen
    \item Landscape the garden
    \item Restore old furniture

\section{Describing Places and Landscapes}

When describing a beautiful place, use strong adjectives to create a picture.

\begin{readingbox}[title=Sample Description: Torres del Paine]
One of the most beautiful places I have ever visited is \textbf{Torres del Paine}. It features rocky, \textbf{snow-covered mountains} located in the south of Chile.
The view of the mountains is \textbf{spellbinding}. It is truly \textbf{breathtaking} to see the peaks against the clear blue sky.
Other features include:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Freshwater lakes
    \item Grassy meadows and rolling hills
    \item Mysterious dense forests
    \item Picturesque churches (e.g., on an island like in Bled)
\end{itemize}
\end{readingbox}

\subsection{Adjectives for Places}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Breathtaking} - Extremely beautiful/impressive.
    \item \textbf{Spellbinding} - Holding your attention completely; fascinating.
    \item \textbf{Picturesque} - Visually attractive (like a picture).
    \item \textbf{Dense} (forest) - Thick, difficult to see through.
    \item \textbf{Rolling} (hills) - Gently rising and falling.
\end{itemize}
