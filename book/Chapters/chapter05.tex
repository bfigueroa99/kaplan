\section{Reading Context}
\begin{readingbox}[title=Dialogue: The Business Proposal]
\textbf{Entrepreneur:} I've developed a new app idea that I believe will be a game-changer in the market.\\
\textbf{Investor:} That sounds interesting. Tell me more about your business model and revenue projections.\\
\textbf{Entrepreneur:} We're looking for a partnership with experienced stakeholders who can help us scale quickly.\\
\textbf{Investor:} What makes your product unique compared to competitors?\\
\textbf{Entrepreneur:} Our unique feature offers solutions that no other competitor currently provides. We have a solid business plan ready.\\
\textbf{Investor:} I like the concept. Let's arrange a meeting with our management team to discuss investment opportunities.
\end{readingbox}

\section{Key Concepts: Business Fundamentals}

\begin{vocabbox}[title=Core Business Vocabulary]
\begin{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Partnership} (noun): A business relationship between two or more people.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Sociedad / Asociación}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Entrepreneur} (noun): A person who starts a business, taking on financial risks in hope of profit.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Emprendedor}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Investor} (noun): A person who puts money into a business expecting profit.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Inversionista}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Stakeholder} (noun): A person with an interest or concern in a business.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Parte interesada}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Revenue} (noun): Income from business operations, typically of substantial amount.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Ingresos}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Business Model} (noun): The strategy for how a company creates and delivers value.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Modelo de negocio}
    \end{itemize}
    \item \keyterm{Competitor} (noun): A company or person trying to achieve the same goals.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item \trans{Competidor}
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}
\end{vocabbox}

\section{Grammar Focus: Using Business Terms in Context}

Business vocabulary often appears in specific grammatical contexts. Here are common patterns:

\begin{grammarbox}[title=Business Expression Patterns]
\textbf{Discussing Ideas:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item We \textbf{have developed} a new product. (Present Perfect for recent ideas)
    \item This solution \textbf{will offer} unique benefits. (Future for projections)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Financial Discussion:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The company \textbf{generates} significant revenue. (Present Simple for facts)
    \item We \textbf{project} 40\% growth next year. (Present for predictions)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Partnership and Collaboration:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item We \textbf{are looking for} partners. (Present Continuous for current action)
    \item Let's \textbf{arrange} a meeting. (Imperative for proposals)
\end{itemize}
\end{grammarbox}

\section{Business Activities and Processes}

\begin{vocabbox}[title=Common Business Activities]
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Negotiate} a contract - Reach agreement on terms
    \item \textbf{Close a deal} - Finalize a business agreement
    \item \textbf{Launch a product} - Introduce new offering to market
    \item \textbf{Scale the business} - Expand operations significantly
    \item \textbf{Conduct market research} - Study customer needs and competition
    \item \textbf{Develop a strategy} - Create long-term business plan
    \item \textbf{Manage cash flow} - Control money in and out of business
    \item \textbf{Pitch an idea} - Present business concept to investors
\end{itemize}
\end{vocabbox}

\section{British English Notes}

In British English, some business terms have different usage:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Turnover} (British) = \textbf{Revenue/Sales} (American) - Total income
    \item \textbf{Trading} (British) = \textbf{Operating} (American) - Conducting business
    \item \textbf{Sackings} (British) = \textbf{Firings} (American) - Job terminations
\end{itemize}
