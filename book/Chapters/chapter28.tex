\section{Grammar Focus: Email Structure and Tone}

\begin{grammarbox}[title=Formal Email Structure]
\textbf{1. Subject Line:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Should be specific and clear
    \item Examples: "Request for Meeting," "Project Update - Q1 Progress"
\end{itemize}

\textbf{2. Greeting:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Formal: "Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name]," "Dear Sir/Madam,"
    \item Less formal: "Hello [Name]," "Hi [Name],"
\end{itemize}

\textbf{3. Opening:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Formal: "I hope this email finds you well."
    \item "I am writing to..." (state purpose)
\end{itemize}

\textbf{4. Body:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Clear paragraphs with one main idea each
    \item Use active voice when possible
    \item Be concise and professional
\end{itemize}

\textbf{5. Closing:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Formal: "Best regards," "Yours sincerely," "Respectfully,"
    \item Less formal: "Best," "Thanks," "Cheers,"
\end{itemize}

\textbf{6. Signature:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Full name
    \item Title
    \item Contact information
\end{itemize}
\end{grammarbox}

\section{Types of Business Emails}

\subsection{1. Request for Information}

\textbf{Pattern:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Opening: State the purpose
    \item Body: Explain what you need and why
    \item Closing: Request timeline and express gratitude
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Example:}

"Dear Ms. Brown, I would appreciate it if you could provide information regarding the updated project specifications. Could you send them by Friday? Thank you in advance."

\subsection{2. Status Update}

\textbf{Pattern:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Opening: State that it's an update
    \item Body: Current progress, achievements, next steps
    \item Closing: Note any pending items or decisions needed
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Example:}

"Hi James, I'm writing to update you on the marketing campaign. We've completed the first draft of the advertisement, and it's ready for your review. Please let me know your feedback by Wednesday."

\subsection{3. Complaint or Problem Resolution}

\textbf{Pattern:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Opening: Acknowledge the relationship
    \item Body: Describe the issue professionally (no blame)
    \item Closing: Propose solutions and request action
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Example:}

"Dear Mr. Chen, Thank you for your business. I wanted to bring a concern to your attention regarding the last shipment. The items arrived damaged. Could we arrange for a replacement? Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter."

\section{[INCORRECT] Common Mistakes in Business Email}

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=red!5, colframe=red!30, title=Email Etiquette Errors]

\textbf{Mistake 1:} Too informal tone with higher authority
\begin{itemize}
    \item [INCORRECT] "Hey boss, whatcha up to?"
    \item [CORRECT] "Dear Sir/Madam, I hope this email finds you well."
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mistake 2:} Missing subject line or vague subject
\begin{itemize}
    \item [INCORRECT] Subject: "Hi"
    \item [CORRECT] Subject: "Request for Project Extension - Deadline Update"
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mistake 3:} Unclear opening
\begin{itemize}
    \item [INCORRECT] "I'm writing about something."
    \item [CORRECT] "I am writing to request approval for the budget proposal."
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mistake 4:} Forgetting attachments or mentioning them incorrectly
\begin{itemize}
    \item [INCORRECT] "Please find the attached document" (but file is missing)
    \item [CORRECT] Make sure to actually attach before sending
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Mistake 5:} Poor grammar or spelling
\begin{itemize}
    \item [INCORRECT] "Their budget needs approval"
    \item [CORRECT] "The budget needs approval" or "Their approval is needed"
\end{itemize}

\end{tcolorbox}

British business emails have distinct characteristics:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Politeness markers:} British emails are often more formal and polite
    \begin{itemize}
        \item "I would be grateful if..." (more British than American)
        \item "Yours faithfully," (traditional British closing)
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Spelling:} Use British English spellings
    \begin{itemize}
        \item colour, organisation, recognise
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Tone:} British writers often use understated language
    \begin{itemize}
        \item "This might be worth considering..." (rather than direct statements)
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Closings:} Common British sign-offs
    \begin{itemize}
        \item "Kind regards," "Yours sincerely," "Cheers," "All the best"
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

\section{Key Takeaways}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Always include a clear subject line
    \item Match your tone (formal/informal) to the recipient and context
    \item Use standard structure: greeting, opening, body, closing, signature
    \item Use appropriate phrases like "I would appreciate," "Could you please," "Look forward to"
    \item Proofread before sending for grammar and spelling errors
    \item Be concise and clear â€” respect the reader's time
    \item Use "I would" and "could you" for polite requests
    \item Always express gratitude appropriately
    \item Follow British conventions if writing to UK audiences
\end{itemize}
